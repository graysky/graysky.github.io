--- title: "Flowtown Acquires WhoShouldiFollow" layout: post ---

I'm pleased to share that Flowtown has acquired the technology behind WhoShouldiFollow, a Twitter friend recommendation engine that my friend and I created. Flowtown is a social marketing platform aimed at helping businesses engage with their customers. What is particularly exciting to me is that the WSIF engine can help Flowtown deliver actionable intelligence for how businesses can expand their network to find new potential customers. Thanks to Ethan Bloch and Dan Martell [1] of Flowtown, both masters of the startup hustle (and blog infographic) who have been great to work with during this process!

My friend Gary Elliott and I originally built WhoShouldiFollow[2] back in early 2008 as a way to make Twitter more useful, and as an opportunity to experiment with collaborative filters.

Notes: - both on twitter since mid-2006 - real-time recommendations
